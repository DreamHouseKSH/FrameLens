How FrameLens Works

FrameLens places a movable transparent capture frame over your Mac screen. Move or resize the frame, choose an image format and save folder, then capture the framed area using the capture button or a global shortcut.

  • Capture exact pixel sizes such as 1280 x 800, 1440 x 900, 2560 x 1600, and custom sizes.
  • Save as PNG, JPEG, or HEIC.
  • Customize filename rules and save folder presets.
  • Use global shortcuts to capture or show and hide the frame.

Screen Recording Permission

macOS requires Screen Recording permission for any app that captures screen content. FrameLens uses this permission only when you trigger a screenshot. It does not record video, monitor your screen in the background, or upload captured images.

To grant permission, open System Settings > Privacy & Security > Screen & System Audio Recording, then allow FrameLens. You may need to quit and reopen the app after changing the permission.

Privacy Policy

FrameLens does not collect, store, sell, analyze, or transmit personal data.

  • Screenshots are saved locally in the app's sandbox container by default, or in a folder explicitly selected by the user.
  • Settings and presets are stored locally on the user's Mac.
  • No analytics or tracking SDKs are used.
  • No captured images are uploaded to any server.

FAQ

Does FrameLens record video?
No. FrameLens captures still images only.

Can I set the exact image size?
Yes. FrameLens uses pixel-based capture sizes and supports custom size presets.

Can I change the output format?
Yes. PNG, JPEG, and HEIC are supported.

Where are screenshots saved?
FrameLens saves screenshots locally in the app's sandbox container by default. If you choose another folder in settings, screenshots are saved to that user-selected folder.
